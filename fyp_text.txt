ABSTRACT  
Agriculture is a major contributor to Pakistan’s economy and a primary source of livelihood for 
rural communities. However, farmers face significant challenges due to limited access to timely 
agricultural information, low literacy levels, poor internet con nectivity, and reliance on 
traditional farming practices. The absence of localized crop guidance, real -time weather updates, 
and scientific soil monitoring leads to inefficient resource usage, low crop yields, and economic 
losses. To address these issues, this project proposes PakFasal, a Flutter -based mobile application 
designed to provide multilingual agricultural learning content, voice -to-text and text -to-speech 
interaction, offline accessibility, and real -time weather forecasting. The system integrates  
affordable IoT -based soil moisture and pH sensors to collect field data and employs a Decision 
Support System (DSS) to generate recommendations for irrigation and fertilizer usage. 
Additionally, the application includes a farmer –company marketplace and a query portal for 
expert or AI -based assistance. The expected outcomes include improved crop productivity, 
optimized water and fertilizer utilization, reduced dependency on intermediaries, and enhanced 
farmer engagement through accessible digital technology . By combining mobile technology, 
voice interfaces, cloud services, and IoT integration, PakFasal aims to empower farmers with 
intelligent tools that promote sustainable agriculture and economic growth.

Introduction  
Agriculture is the backbone of Pakistan’s economy and contributes significantly to the national 
GDP while employing nearly half of the country’s labor force. Rural communities largely depend 
on farming as their primary source of income. However, despite it s importance, the agricultural 
sector still faces numerous challenges such as outdated farming practices, lack of scientific 
knowledge, poor access to timely information, and weak digital infrastructure in remote areas. 
Farmers often rely on traditional me thods passed down through generations, which may not be 
aligned with modern agricultural research and climate realities. This results in low crop yields, 
inefficient water usage, excessive fertilizer application, and economic losses.  
With the advancement of Information and Communication Technology (ICT), particularly 
smartphones and mobile applications, new opportunities have emerged to transform agricultural 
practices. Even in rural areas of Pakistan, smartphone penetration is increas ing. Mobile -based 
solutions can provide farmers with instant access to crop management techniques, weather 
updates, disease detection guidance, and government scheme information. However, many 
existing systems are either text -based, require continuous inte rnet connectivity, or are not 
localized according to regional languages and farming conditions. Illiteracy and low digital 
literacy further limit the usability of such applications.  
The proposed project, PakFasal , aims to bridge this gap by developing a farmer -centric mobile 
application using Flutter. The system will provide multilingual educational content (Urdu and 
English), voice -to-text and text -to-speech support for illiterate farmers, offline accessibility f or 
low-connectivity regions, real -time weather forecasting, and IoT -based soil monitoring (moisture 
and pH sensors). Additionally, the platform will include a farmer –company marketplace to 
eliminate middlemen and a query porta l for expert or AI -driven assistance.  
The main purpose of this project is to empower farmers with accessible knowledge, data -driven 
decision support, and direct market connectivity. By integrating mobile technology, IoT, and 
intelligent recommendation systems, PakFasal aims to enhance agricult ural productivity, reduce 
costs, and promote sustainable farming practices in Pakistan.  
 
Problem Statement  
Farmers in Pakistan face significant challenges due to limited access to timely and localized 
agricultural information such as crop calendars, government schemes, weather updates, and 
scientific irrigation guidelines. Many farmers are illiterate or semi -literate and reside in rural 
areas with weak internet connectivity, which restricts their ability to use existing digital 
agricultural platforms effectively. As a result, they rely heavily on traditional and unscientific 
farming practices, leading to low cro p yields, inefficient use of water and fertilizers, and reduced 
profitability. Current agricultural applications lack offline functionality, voice -based interaction, 
---PAGE---
and integration with affordable IoT sensors for real -time soil monitoring. Therefore, there is a 
critical need for a comprehensive, multilingual, partially offline, and IoT -integrated mobile -based 
decision support system tailored specifically to the needs of Pakistani farmers.  
Proposed Solution  
To address the challenges faced by farmers in Pakistan, this project proposes the development of 
PakFasal , a Flutter -based mobile application designed to provide localized agricultural 
education and intelligent decision support. The application will offer multilingual tutorials, crop 
calendars, and videos to ensure accessibility across different regions. It w ill integrate voice -to-
text and text -to-speech functionality to support illiterate and semi -literate farmers. Real -time 
weather forecasting APIs will provide timely alerts and recommendations for irrigation and 
planting decisions. Additionally, low -cost Io T-based soil moisture and pH sensors will collect 
real-time field data and transmit it to a cloud -based backend system. A Decision Support System 
(DSS) will analyze this data to generate practical recommendations for irrigation and fertilizer 
usage. Offlin e data storage will ensure accessibility in low -connectivity areas. The platform will 
also include a farmer –company marketplace and a query portal for expert or AI -driven 
assistance, creating a unified, farmer -centered digital solution.

Project Scope  
The scope of the PakFasal project clearly defines the boundaries of the system by specifying 
what will be included in the development and what will not be covered within this academic 
project. The primary goal of this project is to design and develop a fun ctional prototype of a 
mobile -based agricultural support system targeted specifically at small and medium -scale 
farmers in rural Pakistan. The system will focus on improving access to agricultural knowledge, 
enabling data -driven farming decisions, and redu cing dependence on intermediaries through 
digital connectivity.  
The project will include the development of a cross -platform mobile application using Flutter, 
primarily optimized for Android devices. The application will provide multilingual agricultural 
content, including crop calendars, tutorials, and videos tailored  to local farming practices. It will 
integrate voice -to-text and text -to-speech functionality to ensure accessibility for illiterate and 
semi -literate farmers. Real -time weather forecasting APIs will be incorporated to provide 
location -based weather update s and alerts. The scope also includes integration of affordable IoT -
based soil moisture and pH sensors using ESP32 or Arduino  microcontrollers to collect real -
time field data. A cloud -based backend using Firebase services will handle user authentication, 
data storage, synchronization, and sensor data management. A basic Decision Support System 
(DSS) will analyze soil and weather  data to provide simple, rule -based recommendations for 
irrigation and fertilizer usage. Additionally, the project will include a farmer –company 
marketplace module for direct communication and a query portal where farmers can submit 
agricultural issues via  text, voice, or images.  
The project will ensure offline accessibility for essential features such as educational content and 
crop calendars to support farmers in low -connectivity areas. However, the scope is limited to 
prototype -level implementation and controlled pilot testing. Large -scale commercial deployment, 
nationwide hardware distribution, advanced machine learning -based disease detection models, 
financial transaction systems, and direct integration with government databases are excluded 
from the scope of this academic proj ect. The focus remains on delivering a working, scalable 
prototype that demonstrates feasibility, usability, and alignment with the identified problem.  
 
Software Development Lifecycle (SDLC) Model:  
To ensure systematic, organized, and professional development of the PakFasal system, the Agile 
Software Development Lifecycle (SDLC) model will be adopted. Agile is selected because the 
project consists of multiple independent yet interconnected modules s uch as the mobile 
application, IoT sensor integration, weather API services, voice interaction module, cloud 
backend, decision support system (DSS), marketplace, and query portal. These modules can be 
developed in small increments (sprints) and integrated gradually to form the complete system. 
Agile provides flexibility to refine requirements, test features early, gather feedback, and make 
---PAGE---
improvements without major redesign costs, which is suitable for an academic project with 
evolving requirements.  
Agile Methodology  
The PakFasal system will be developed in short iterative cycles known as sprints. Each sprint 
will focus on a specific module such as user authentication, multilingual content delivery, voice 
processing, weather integration, IoT connectivity, DSS implement ation, or marketplace 
functionality. Every sprint will include all SDLC stages including requirement refinement, 
design, implementation, testing, and integration. After completing each sprint, performance 
metrics such as system response time, sensor accura cy, usability feedback, and functionality 
validation will be evaluated. This iterative feedback mechanism ensures continuous improvement 
and better alignment with user needs.  
Requirement Analysis  
In this phase, all functional and non -functional requirements of PakFasal will be gathered and 
documented. Functional requirements include multilingual content delivery, voice -based 
interaction, IoT sensor data integration, weather updates, marketplace com munication, and query 
handling. Non -functional requirements include performance efficiency, system reliability, 
usability, scalability, and offline accessibility. Specific targets such as acceptable response time, 
minimum sensor accuracy, and usability ben chmarks will also be defined to measure project 
success.  
System and Architecture Design  
During this phase, the overall system architecture will be designed using a layered client -server 
architecture integrated with IoT components. The Flutter mobile application will serve as the 
presentation layer, Firebase services will act as the backend an d data layer, and IoT devices will 
collect environmental data. Data flow between modules, database schemas, API communication, 
and integration points will be clearly defined. UI/UX mockups will also be created to ensure the 
application remains simple and farmer friendly . 
Implementation  
The implementation phase will follow incremental development based on defined sprints. The 
mobile application will be developed using Flutter, while Firebase will handle authentication, 
data storage, and synchronization. IoT sensors (soil moisture and pH) will be connected through 
ESP32 or Arduino and integrated with the cloud using MQTT or HTTP protocols. Weather APIs 
and voice services will be integrated progressively. Each module will be developed, tested, and 
then integrated with the overall system.

Testing  
Testing will be performed continuously throughout development. Unit testing will verify 
individual components such as sensor data collection or API responses. Integration testing will 
ensure smooth communication between mobile  applications , backend services, and IoT devices.

Post-deployment, system logs, user feedback, and performance metrics will be analyzed to

improvement cycle ensures that PakFasal remains effective, scalable, and aligned with the needs 
of Pakistani farmers.

Litera ture Review  
2.1 Literature Review  
The emergence of Information and Communications Technology (ICT), Artificial Intelligence 
(AI), Internet of Things (IoT), and mobile computing in the recent past have had a major impact 
on the way agriculture was practiced in the past. Traditional agricult ure in developing nations 
such as Pakistan is based on the traditional knowledge, manual observations, and guesses in 
irrigation and the use of fertilizers. Such activities result in very low crop production, poor usage 
of resources, and lack of stability in the economy. As the AI has evolved at a very high pace, 
Decision Support Systems (DSS), mobile applications, and IoT -based monitoring systems, 
agriculture is slowly becoming data -driven and smart farming methods.  
This part entails a critical literature overview of available studies and technology solutions on the 
agricultural advisory systems, IoT -focused precision farming, crop intelligence -based on 
weather, voice -enhanced interface systems and farmer marketplace systems. It also identifies 
their methodologies, accomplishments, and setbacks to support the necessity of PakFasal.  
2.1.1.1 Mobile -Based Agricultural Advisory Systems.  
TATA Trusts launched mKRISHI [1], an agricultural advisory service based on mobile phones to 
enable farmers access experts. The system offers crop advice, market price and advisory services 
via SMS and mobile applications. The paper showed increased interaction between the experts in 
the field of agriculture and farmers.  
This system is, however, primarily text -based and needs a stable internet connection. It lacks 
offline services and voice services to illiterate farmers. Besides, it does not have real -time sensor 
interfaces and smart DSS suggestions.  
PakFasal builds upon this effort by combining the support of two voices, having an offline 
presence, soil monitoring via IoT integration, and suggestions provided by AI and implemented 
into a single mobile platform.  
2.1.2 Smart Irrigation Systems based on IoT.  
Smart irrigation systems based on IoT aim to optimize irrigation time based on the soil moisture 
sensors and environmental monitoring systems [2]. Such systems gather real -time data and 
process the water use plans to enhance the conservation and productivity of water.  
The systems though effective in precision farming are costly and they demand technical expertise 
in installation and service. Most of the implementations are made to large -scale commercial 
farms and not to the small -holder farmers.  
PakFasal overcomes this bottleneck by incorporating low -cost soil moisture and pH sensors with 
ESP32/Arduino modules, which allows the small -scale farmers in Pakistan to adopt IoT.  
---PAGE---
2.1.3 Decision Support Systems (DSS) in Agriculture.  
Another famous agricultural DSS model is DSSAT (Decision Support System Agrotechnology 
Transfer) that can be applied to crop simulation, yield prediction, and management planning  [3]. 
It uses environmental, soil and crop data sets to come up with recommendations that are 
scientifically sound.  
However, DSSAT is complicated, and it is not very user -friendly with researchers and 
agricultural specialists in mind. It needs technical skills and extensive data, which low -literate 
farmers could not access.  
PakFasal is a simplified form of DSS, which incorporates the logic of making recommendations 
directly into the interface of a mobile application that uses weather API and IoT sensors to input 
data, and thereby allows farmers to make scientific decisions.  
2.1.4 Agricultural Information Systems based on weather.  
The advisory systems based on weather are used in helping farmers to make irrigation, 
harvesting decisions, and managing pests  [4]. Climate information Services Mobile climate 
information services offer forecast - oriented advice.  
Nevertheless, the majority of the current weather apps provide general forecasting and not crop 
and location -specific actionable advice. They fail to integrate data about soil, stage of crops, and 
farmers.  
PakFasal takes weather APIs and mixes them with the soil sensor data, crop calendars to produce 
actionable and region -focused recommendations, as opposed to a mere weather update.  
2.1.5 Voice -Based Interfaces to Low Literacy Users.  
Studies on Interactive voice Response (IVR) systems have demonstrated that voice interfaces 
enhance accessibility among low -literate groups to a great extent [5].  
The majority of IVR systems are controlled by menus, though, and are inflexible. They are not 
embedded with AI -driven recommendation systems, IoT networks, and intelligent crop 
counselling systems.  
PakFasal combines voice -to-text and text -to-speech to directly be part of the agricultural 
decision -making pipeline, allowing illiterate farmers to talk to their computer.  
2.1.6 Mobile Applications Empowering Smallholder Farmers  
According to recent research, there is a recognition of the role of mobile applications in 
empowerment of the smallholder farmers by providing advisory services and accessing market 
[6]. These applications enhance communication to the farmers, and less reliance on 
intermediaries.

• Offline functionality  
• Integrated IoT support  
• AI-based DSS  
• Multilingual support  
Single market between farmers and companies.  
PakFasal combines all these functions into a single ecology that is farmer friendly . 
2.1.7 Retrieval -Augmented Generation (RAG) in Agricultural Advisory Systems  
Retrieval -Augmented Generation (RAG) is a new AI framework that integrates information 
retrieval processes with large language models (LLMs) to enhance natural language processing 
tasks with a knowledge -intensive nature  [7]. RAG does not only use pre -trained information, but 
accesses the relevant information stored in the external knowledge base and utilizes it as 
grounding before the generation of responses. Old -fashioned AI chat systems only create a 
response based on mode l training data, and this can result in hallucinations or out -of-date 
information. RAG -based systems enhance accuracy in fact by retrieving domain -specific content, 
and generate responses then, eliminating the risk of hallucinations [8]. 
Recent studies show that RAG can greatly enhance domain specific question answering and 
particularly in domains with much knowledge e.g. healthcare, finance and agriculture [9]. 
Similarity -based document matching is further improved with dense semantic retrieval methods 
like Dense Passage Retrieval (DPR) to extract accurate information [10]. Nevertheless, most 
agricultural advisory systems are based on either a fixed FAQ matching or separate AI model 
without any dynamic retrieval.  
PakFasal incorporates a query handling framework based on RAGs in its Query Portal, where 
queries by farmers are transformed into embeddings and compared to an organized agricultural 
knowledge base with semantic similarity queries [11], [12]. The documents that have been 
retrieved are then fed into a language model to produce grounded and context -related responses. 
This method allows to minimize misinformation, allows increasing reliability, and makes sure 
that responses are backed by verifia ble agricultural knowledge.

Although the innovations in smart agriculture technologies are made, there are still several  
significant gaps in the process of creating intelligent, farmer -centered agricultural support 
systems. These gaps are pointed out in the following section and how PakFasal handles them.

Existing Limitations:  
Most agricultural systems concentrate on one functionality (advisory services, IoT monitoring, 
weather forecasting, or accessing the marketplace). No single platform exists in which all these 
services are incorporated into an ecosystem.  
The way PakFasal will fill this Gap:  
• Integrates IoT soil sensor, weather prediction, DSS suggestions and marketplace . 
• Offers a mobile one stop shop service to farmers.  
2.2.2 Restricted Access by the illiterate farmers.  
Existing Limitations:

intelligent advisory modules.  
The way PakFasal fills this Gap:  
• Combines voice to text and text to speech applications.  
• Bilingual (Urdu/English) services.  
• Allows free intercourse without reading writing addiction.

Existing Limitations:  
Even most of the systems need constant connection to the internet that is not reliable in rural

The way of handling this Gap by PakFasal:

2.2.4 Precision Systems based on IoT are very expensive.  
Existing Limitations:  
None of the current smart farming systems are affordable and are targeted at large farms.  
The way PakFasal can fill this Gap:

Existing Limitations:  
Weather applications and advisory services do not offer action -oriented specifically crop -oriented

• Creates region -specific  action  recommendations.

Existing Limitations:  
Majority of the agricultural advisory applications are based on:  
• Static FAQ matching  
• Rule-based responses  
• Simple chatbot models

2.3 Requirement Elicitation  
Requirement elicitation refers to the systematic process of identifying, analyzing, and 
documenting the needs of stakeholders and system constraints for the PakFasal platform. Since 
PakFasal is a multi -component intelligent agricultural system integrating IoT sensors, cloud 
services, mobile application development, Decision Support System (DSS), Retrieval -
Augmented Generation (RAG), and multilingual voice interaction, a structured  approach was 
necessary to ensure completeness and feasibility.  
The requirement gathering process combined analysis of existing agricultural advisory platforms, 
stakeholder consultations, domain research in rural agricultural environments of Pakistan, and 
technical feasibility analysis of AI and IoT integration.  
The following subsections describe the elicitation approach in detail.  
 
2.3.1 Requirement Gathering Methods  
2.3.1.1 Document Analysis  
Various research papers, technical reports, and agricultural advisory platforms were analyzed to 
understand existing solutions and their limitations. Studies on IoT -based smart irrigation systems, 
mobile agricultural advisory applications, and AI -based kno wledge retrieval systems were 
reviewed.  
---PAGE---
The analysis revealed several limitations in existing systems:  
• Most agricultural mobile applications require continuous internet connectivity.  
• IoT-based irrigation systems are often expensive and technically complex for small -scale 
farmers.  
• Very few systems integrate multilingual voice interfaces for illiterate farmers.  
• Existing advisory platforms provide static FAQ -based responses instead of dynamic AI -
assisted answers.  
• There is minimal integration of Retrieval -Augmented Generation (RAG) for domain -
specific agricultural knowledge retrieval.  
The document analysis helped identify gaps in accessibility, scalability, affordability, and

2.3.1.2 Stakeholder Need Analysis  
The primary stakeholders of PakFasal include:  
• Small and medium -scale farmers  
• Agricultural extension officers  
• Input suppliers (fertilizer, pesticide, seed companies)  
• Government agricultural bodies  
Through informal discussions, observation of rural farming practices, and analysis of agricultural 
challenges in Pakistan, the following stakeholder needs were identified:  
• Farmers require localized agricultural content in Urdu and regional languages.  
• Illiterate or semi -literate farmers require voice -based interaction instead of text -heavy 
interfaces.  
• Farmers need real -time weather forecasting and soil condition analysis for informed 
decision -making.  
• Farmers require direct communication with verified suppliers to avoid exploitation by 
middlemen.  
• Farmers need instant and reliable answers to crop disease and pest -related queries.  
• Offline accessibility is essential due to weak internet connectivity in rural areas.  
These needs were translated into concrete functional and non -functional requirements.

2.3.1.3 Technical Constraint Analysis  
Technical constraints were analyzed to ensure the feasibility of implementation within available 
resources and infrastructure.  
Key constraints include:  
• Limited processing capabilities of low -cost IoT hardware (ESP32/Arduino).  
• Internet instability in rural areas.  
• Hosting limitations for large -scale AI models.  
• Data synchronization challenges  offline storage and cloud backend.  
• Requirement for lightweight yet efficient RAG -based retrieval mechanism.  
The system architecture was therefore designed to use:  
• Firebase Firestore for cloud storage  
• Offline persistence for local caching  
• Lightweight vector database for RAG retrieval  
• API-based LLM integration instead of full model hosting  
These constraints shaped the modular and scalable design of PakFasal.  
 
2.3.1.4 RAG -Based Knowledge Integration Analysis  
Since PakFasal now integrates Retrieval -Augmented Generation (RAG), specific requirement 
analysis was conducted for AI -based query handling.  
The RAG module is designed to:  
• Retrieve relevant agricultural knowledge from a structured knowledge base.  
• Use vector embeddings to match farmer queries with relevant documents.  
• Generate grounded responses using a Large Language Model (LLM).  
• Reduce hallucination by anchoring responses to retrieved content.  
• Continuously update the FAQ database with validated responses.  
This ensures domain -specific, accurate, and context -aware AI assistance instead of generic 
responses.

System Design  
This chapter describes the system design of the proposed PakFasal intelligent agricultural 
support system . The design explains how different components of the system interact with each 
other to provide agricultural knowledge, weather insights, IoT -based soil monitoring, and 
farmer –company marketplace services.  
3.1 Use Case Design  
Use case design explains the way various users use the PakFasal system and the way the system 
delivers services to its users. It assists in comprehending the system functionality in the eyes of 
the user and finds out the key interactions between the users and the application.  
The PakFasal  system is developed as an agricultural support system that is implemented on 
mobile phones  and helps farmers to find information on agriculture, control the field conditions 
with the help of the IoT sensors and get the chances to receive weather predictions and to 
communicate directly with the agricultural suppliers. The system also enables far mers to ask any 
queries related to crops and get answers from agricultural specialists or intelligent systems.  
Using case diagram  helps in identifying the key actors in the system and functionalities that can 
be availed to them. These actors engage the mobile application and the backend services to 
execute various tasks.  
The key participants of the system of PakFasal are  Farmers, Agricultural Experts, 
Suppliers/Companies, and System Administrators . All the actors had roles and functions in 
the system.  
3.1.1 Actors  
Farmer:  
The PakFasal application is mostly used by the farmer. The system allows farmers to get access 
to agricultural information, keep track of the soil condition, and get advice on how to enhance 
the productivity of their crops.  
The following are the measures that can be undertaken by the farmers:  
• Open and create an account with the mobile application.  
• Get access to agricultural tutorials and articles and videos.  
• See crop schedules and schedules of approved farming.  
• Use the sensor of soil moisture and pH.  
• Check weather predictions of their region.  
• Place orders concerning farming or disease of crops.  
• Post crop pictures to be diagnosed.  
• Shop farm products on the market.  
---PAGE---
• Interaction with suppliers.  
The PakFasal application is expected to be easy and user -friendly and should have the voice 
input and multilingual features to make sure that even semi -literate farmers can use the system 
without any difficulties.  
Agricultural Expert:  
Agricultural professionals help farmers through professional advice associated with crop 
management, pests, irrigation methods, and fertilizer applications.  
They are responsible for:  
• Reviewing farmer queries  
• Giving professional answers and solutions.  
• Revising agricultural knowledge base.  
• Assistance to the farmers in diagnosing diseases.  
The professionals assist in provision of fair and valid information to farmers to enhance 
agricultural productivity.  
Supplier / Agricultural Company:  
The farming inputs supplied through the PakFasal marketplace module are suppliers or 
agricultural companies, who supply farming inputs in terms of seeds, fertilizers, pesticides, and 
farming equipment.  
The suppliers can do the following:  
• Create and maintain product listing.  
• Offer the product information and costs.  
• Interact with farmers directly.  
• Getting orders or product inquiries.  
Marketplace option assists in the removal of intermediaries and farmers can buy farm products 
directly out of the farms.  
System Administrator:  
System administrator takes care of the general operations of PakFasal platform. The 
administrator will make sure that the system is functioning properly, and that data and resources 
are in place as well.  
Responsibilities include:  
• User account management/ System access.  
• Enhancing performance and activity logs of the monitoring systems.  
---PAGE---
• Knowledge resource and content in agriculture.  
• Managing system changes and maintenance.  
• Managing database records  
The administrator will make the platform secure, reliable, and up to date.  
3.1.2 Use Case Description  
PakFasal system does offer several essential features which were intended to assist farmers in 
their day -to-day operations on the farm.  
User Authentication:  
The application requires users to create an account and log in to the application after which they 
can access system features. Firebase Authentication is used to authenticate the user and provide 
them with secure access to the system.  
Access of Agriculture Education Material:  
Farmers will have access to numerous educational contents such as articles, tutorials and videos 
on how to manage crops, the methods of irrigation and disease prevention. It provides content in 
different languages to enhance accessibility.  
Crop Calendar Access:  
The crop calendar module gives information on the planting, irrigation, fertilization, and 
harvesting time of crops specific to a region. This assists the farmers to organize their 
agricultural practices better.  
Weather Forecast Module:  
The system incorporates weather APIs in offering real -time weather predictions. Growers can see 
the temperature, humidity, precipitation, and wind speed to take sound decisions regarding the 
farming process.  
IoT Sensor Monitoring:  
PakFasal system is an integration of the soil moisture sensors and soil pH sensors which are 
linked with ESP32 or Arduino microcontrollers. The sensor data is sent to the cloud and is shown 
in the smart phone application.  
This enables farmers to check the level of fields and control the process of irrigation.  
Query Handling System:  
Agriculture will be able to send the requests connected with the diseases of crops, the 
infestations of pests or agricultural methods. One is able to send queries through text, voice input 
or even pictures.  
---PAGE---
These queries are sent to the system by the agriculture experts or intelligent decision support 
modules. When the response has been generated, it is sent back to the farmer via the mobile 
application.  
Farmer -Company Marketplace:  
The marketplace module enables the farmers to view agricultural products, including seeds, 
fertilizer and pesticides. The farmers can interact directly with the suppliers in order to gain 
information on the products and make purchases.  
This allows minimization of the need to utilize mediators and allows agricultural markets to be 
more transparent.  
Recommendations of the decision support system:  
The system uses sensory, weather and crop knowledge to give guidance to farmers. Some of 
these recommendations can incorporate the irrigation time, the application of fertilizer, and 
disease control methods.  
 
3.2 Activity Diagram : 
The activity diagram will display the general process of PakFasal system and how a farmer is 
involved with the various modules of the system.  
Initialization The initial stage of the application is called the initialization stage.  
The first step is the activation of the PakFasal mobile application by the farmer. The system 
verifies the user registration or not. Unless the user is registered, the user is forced to complete 
the registration process before he or she can have access to the system.  
Once Firebase Authentication is completed successfully, the farmer will have access to the main 
dashboard of the application.  
3.2.2 Farmer Interaction of System Modules.  
Upon logging in, the farmer can browse through various applications modules of the application 
such as:  
• Educational resources  
• Crop calendar  
• Weather forecasting  
• Sensor monitoring  
• Marketplace  
• Query handling portal  
---PAGE---
The modules offer certain functionalities that help farmers make informed decisions related to 
agriculture.  
3.2.3 IoT Sensor Data Collection:  
This subsystem is the IoT which is the soil moisture sensor and the soil pH sensor that is linked 
to microcontrollers like ESP32 or Arduino.  
These sensors check the soil condition in field on a continuous basis. The microcontroller takes 
sensor measurements and sends them to the cloud via some communication protocols like 
MQTT or HTTP.  
The sensor data is also stored in Firebase Firestore whereby mobile application can retrieve it.  
3.2.4 Weather Data Processing:  
External weather APIs are used to get the level of weather information, and these include open 
weather map. The application forwards the request to the weather API that provides real -time 
weather conditions such as the temperature, humidity, and rainfall p robability, and wind speed.  
This fact assists farmers with an irrigation schedule, planting and harvesting time.  
3.2.5 Query Handling Process:  
The farmers can make inquiries concerning crops disease, irrigation, manure or pest control. The 
queries may be placed with the help of:  
• Text input  
• V oice input  
The query is fed into the system and passed on to the agricultural experts, or AI -based models.  
Then, the generated response is returned to the farmer on the mobile application.  
3.2.6 Decision Support System:  
The decision support system (DSS) is used to analyze sensor data, weather data and crop data to 
give recommendations to the farmers.  
The DSS can offer the following recommendations:  
• Irrigation scheduling  
• Fertilizer application  
• Prevention methods of diseases.  
• Crop selection guidance  
These suggestions enable farmers to come up with data -driven farming decisions.

3.3.1 Farmer Request Processing:  
As soon as a farmer takes some action via the mobile application, the request is forwarded to the 
back -end server.  

---PAGE---

components including:  
• Firebase database  
• IoT sensors  
• Weather APIs  
Once the request has been processed, the system will then respond to the mobile application.  
3.3.2 Sensor Data Retrieval:  
The mobile application periodically asks the cloud database to provide sensor information. The 
backend gets the most recent readings of soil moisture and pH sensors that are stored in Firebase.  
The information is then forwarded to the mobile app where it is presented in the graphic form to 
farmers.  
3.3.3 Retrieving the weather information:  
On opening the weather module, the mobile application makes a request for the weather API. 
The API has real -time weather data that is shown to the farmer in the application.  
3.4 Software Architecture Diagram:  
PakFasal system has an architecture which is layered to guarantee modularity and scalability. 
The architecture is separated into several layers with each layer being charged with certain 
system functions.  
3.4.1 Presentation Layer:  
The mobile application that will be developed in Flutter is the presentation layer.  
The application offers an easy -to-use interface which covers:  
• Multilingual content  
• V oice interaction  
• Visual data representation  
• Offline learning resources  
The mobile application is the main interface between the farmer and the system.  
3.4.2 Application Layer:  
Handles system logic and data processing are in the application layer.  
This layer includes:  
• User management through firebase authentication.  
• Node.js backend APIs  
---PAGE---
• Decision Support System  
• Query processing system  
Application layer is used to process user requests and interacts with data layer.  
3.4.3 Data Layer:  
Storing and maintenance of system data is the task of the data layer.  
Key data components include:  
• Farmer profiles  
• Sensor readings  
• The content of agricultural knowledge.  
• Products listing within the marketplace.  
• Query records  
Firebase Firestore is taken as the major cloud database to store the data of the system.  
3.4.4 IoT Integration Layer:  
List of hardware sensors and microcontrollers used to collect real -time environmental data make 
up the IoT integration layer.  
The sensors sense the moisture content in the soil and pH levels and transmit the data to the 
cloud data base.  
This integration enables the system to give precise field information to the farmers.

3.7 Collaboration Diagram  
The collaboration diagram depicts the communication between various components of the 
PakFasal system that allows the system to carry out the operations. It concentrates on the 
communication between system modules at running time and emphasizes the passing  of 
information between mobile applications, the backend services, the IoT devices, the databases 
and the artificial intelligence components.  
The PakFasal system incorporates among various technologies a Flutter mobile application, a 
cloud database, IoT sensors, weather APIs, and query processing modules that are based on AI. 
These elements work together to give farmers real -time agricultural da ta, smart suggestions and 
availability of educational materials.  
The collaboration diagram depicts the interaction of this system components to a request sent by 
the farmer and provides the right response.  
3.7.1 The elements of Collaboration Diagram:  
PakFasal collaboration diagram comprises of the following major components.  
Farmer (User)  
The system involves the farmer who is the main player of the system and the one who 
communicates with the PakFasal application via a smartphone. Among the things that farmers 
can do, are viewing crop calendars, accessing tutorials, checking weather predict ions, monitoring 
soil condition, making queries in relation to crops and browsing products available in the 
marketplace.  
The farmer is the one who initiates all interactions with the system using the mobile application.  
Mobile Interface (Flutter Interface):  
The PakFasal system is operated through the mobile application that primarily acts as a point of 
contact between the farmers and the system. It was created with Flutter to offer a cross -platform 
mobile application.  
The functions of the application are as follows:  
• Forwards user requests to the back -end services.  
• Shows product facts and informational materials.  
• Displays sensor data and weather prediction.  
• It enables farmers to make queries either in text, voice, or picture.  
• Shows recommendations that are created by the system.  
The mobile app is connected to the back -end services via safe API requests.  
---PAGE---
Node.js / Cloud Services (Backend Server):  
The back server handles interactions among mobile applications, databases, Internet of sensors 
and artificial intelligence services. It manages business logic, request processing and 
synchronization of data.  
The major roles of the backend server are:  
• Authentication/authorization of users.  
• Processing farmer requests  
• Controllability of sensor data retrieval.  
• Getting weather information via APIs.  
• Dealing with AI -generated query responses.  
• Running of activities in the marketplace.  
The backend server is the hub of control of the system.  
IoT Sensor System:  
The subsystem of the IoT gathers environmental data on the agricultural field. It is made up of 
hardware equipment such as:  
• Soil moisture sensors  
• Soil pH sensors  
• ESP32 or Arduino microcontrollers.  
These sensors keep on checking the conditions of soils and relay sensor data to the cloud by 
connecting through communication protocols like MQTT or HTTP . 
The sensor data obtained is stored in the database and the system uses this data to come up with 
irrigation and soil management related recommendations.  
Weather API Service:  
The weather information is significant in agricultural decision making. The PakFasal application 
combines external weather API to get real -time weather forecasts like OpenWeatherMap.  
The weather information that is accessed by the backend server includes:  
• Temperature  
• Humidity  
• Rainfall probability  
• Wind speed  
Such information is sent to the mobile application, and farmers can see weather updates and get 
farming recommendations depending on the weather conditions.  
---PAGE---
Firebase Cloud Database:  
The PakFasal system relies on firebase services to synchronize real -time data and manage users.  
The components of Firebase utilized in the system are:  
• Firebase Authentication that handles user registration and login.  
• Firebase Firestore can be used to store real -time data like sensor readings, user queries, 
and notifications.  
• Firebase storage of multimedia data in form of tutorial videos, images and documents.  
Firebase is known to offer rapid access to data in real time and guarantees seamless 
communication between the mobile app and cloud -based services.  
 
MongoDB Database:  
MongoDB is employed as the main database of structured and large -scale application 
information. MongoDB is used to keep data on system content and AI modules.  
The information stored in MongoDB comprises:  
• Knowledge base in Agriculture.  
• Crop disease information  
• Historical farmer queries  
• AI training datasets  
MongoDB is a document -based  storage which is flexible and it enables easy storage of large data 
sets needed to operate the PakFasal system.  
RAG -Based AI System:  
PakFasal system uses Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG) framework to give smart 
responses to queries of farmers. RAG system is performed by integrating a knowledge database 
and a language model to generate an accurate response.  
The workflow includes:  
• The farmer makes a query by using the mobile application.  
• The query is sent to the RAG system by the backend server.  
• The RAG module gets access to appropriate agricultural knowledge stored in the 
MongoDB.  
• The retrieved information is pushed to a refined language model.  
• The model results in a contextualized response.  
• This is relayed to the server and forwarded to the mobile application.  
---PAGE---
• The method will make sure that the answers are made based on confirmed agricultural 
knowledge and not generic text generation.  
• Fine-Tuned Language Model  
• The system incorporates a fine -tuned language model that enhances accuracy and 
relevance of the responses that are given to farmers.  
The agricultural data used to train the model is:  
• Crop disease descriptions  
• Fertilizer recommendations  
• Soil management practices  
• Irrigation techniques  
• Weather dependent agricultural advice.  
Fine-tuning enables the model to be specialized in agricultural knowledge and it generates more 
accurate and relevant responses to farmers.  
3.7.2 Collaboration Workflow  
This activity of collaboration between system components is the following one:  
1. The PakFasal mobile application is used by the farmer and the request is entered.  
2. The request is forwarded by the mobile application to the backend server.  
3. The server in the background takes the request and decides which system module to use 
in addressing the request.  
4. In the case of sensor monitoring request, the backend will go to Firebase to retrieve 
sensor data.  
5. In case the request deals with weather information, the backend gets real -time weather 
information in the Weather API.  
6. In case the farmer makes an agricultural inquiry, the request is sent to the RAG system by 
the backend.  
7. RAG system receives pertinent agricultural data based on MongoDB.  
8. The language model fine -tuning system takes the information retrieved and produces a 
response.  
9. The mobile application sends responses through the backend server.  
10. The results are presented to the farmer on the mobile application.  
The PakFasal system through this collaborative process offers real time information to the 
farmers, intelligent recommendations and interactive agricultural advice.

5.1 Work Breakdown Structure (WBS)  
A Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) is a project broken down into small and manageable tasks 
in a hierarchical manner. It facilitates the planning of the development, delegation of duties 
among the team members and monitoring the progress of each aspect of th e project. The 
PakFasal project has several stages of the development process which consists of engineering, 
system design, mobile application development, backend integration, artificial intelligence 
integration, IoT integration, testing, and deployment.  
The initial stage of the project is on Requirement Engineering , which involves development of 
the system requirements based on literature review, and analysis of the available agricultural 
systems and knowledge of the farmers in Pakistan. Multilingual support, voice interactive, 
weather information, IoT integration, and offl ine availability are considered as functional 
requirements and non -functional requirements and recorded.  
The second stage is System design  which designs the architecture of PakFasal system. In this 
step, UML notations like case diagrams, activity diagrams, sequence diagrams, class diagrams, 
database diagrams, and collaboration diagrams are developed to capture the system structure and 
system component interactions among system compone nts. Mockups of user interfaces are also 
done to make sure that the application is easy and simple to use by farmers.  
The third stage will consist of Frontend Development , in which the mobile application interface 
will be created with the Flutter framework. The interface is crafted in such a way that it has easy 
navigation, big icons and supports multiple languages to enable farmers who have no or little 
technical knowledg e to use it.  
The fourth stage is Backend Development  which involves the use of Firebase to implement 
cloud services. Firebase Firestore is employed to store user data and sensor data, whereas 
Firebase Authentication is utilized to perform a secure login and registration.  
The fifth step is Database Development  in which the database schema is developed to store 
farmer profiles, crop data, sensor data, learning content, and farmer queries.  
The 6th stage is NLP and V oice Interaction, where voice to text and text to voice capabilities are 
installed to assist the illiterate / semi -literate farmers.  
The seventh stage involves Artificial Intelligence Integration; a Retrieval Augmented 
Generation (RAG) system and a fine -tuned language model  are adopted to deliver an 
intelligent response to a query of a farmer.  
The eighth step is IoT Sensor Integration , whereby the soil moisture and pH sensors are 
attached to ESP32 microcontrollers, which measure the environment of agricultural fields.  
The ninth stage involves System Integration,  which involves the integration of all modules of 
the system such as mobile application, AI services, backend services, APIs, and IoT devices into 
a system.  
At last, the project passes to the Testing and Deployment stages, during which the system is 
tested with the help of different testing methods and deployed to be used by farmers.

5.4 Implementation Details  
The PakFasal system is applied in the form of an interconnected smart agriculture platform, 
consisting of mobile applications, cloud computing, artificial intelligence, and IoT devices 
together and enables farmers to make improved agricultural choices.  
 
Mobile Application Implementation:  
The mobile application is built on the Flutter framework, the codes of which allow mobile 
development across all platforms with a shared codebase. Flutter contains high performance and 
reactive user interface and can be used in Android devices that are fre quently used by the 
farmers.  
The mobile app will have several modules that include:  
• Registration and authentication of the user.  
• Educational resources, educational articles.  
• Crop calendar guidance  
• Weather forecast display  
• Visualization of IoT sensors data.  
• Marketplace access  
• Portal of query submission at Farmer.  
The interface will be user -friendly and straightforward so that farmers can find it easy to navigate 
the system without having to be highly digital.  
Voice Interaction Module:  
V oice interaction is facilitated by providing features of voice interaction with the help of Google 
Speech -to-Text and Text -to-Speech APIs. The speech recognition system issues voice commands 
that farmers can use to make a query that is converted into text . A text -to-speech feature can also 
read out responses in the system.  
This is especially beneficial to the farmers who might not be good readers and writers.  
Cloud Implementation and Backend:  
---PAGE---
Firebase cloud services are used to implement the backend services of PakFasal. Firebase 
Firestore is utilized as the main database to store the information about farmers, data about crops, 
sensor readings, and records of queries.  
Firebase Authentication is employed to offer secure log -in support by the use of either the phone 
number or email authentication. Firebase storage is employed to save multi media files like 
tutorial videos and images.  
Such cloud services offer scalability, reliability and real time data synchronization between the 
mobile application and the database.  
IoT Sensor Integration:  
The system has IoT gadgets that will monitor the soil condition in real time. ESP32 
microcontrollers in agricultural fields have soil moisture sensors and pH sensors attached to 
them.  The sensors constantly record the environmental parameters including the levels of soil 
moisture and PH. These readings are sent to the cloud database using the MQTT or the HTTP 
communication protocols by the ESP32 microcontroller.  
The mobile app pulls the sensor data in the Firebase and presents it as graphs or dashboards so 
that farmers can see the state of soil and make reasonable decisions regarding irrigation and the 
use of fertilizers.  Weather Forecast Integration is the second category of economic services.  
The OpenWeatherMap provides weather data via the API. The mobile application shows such 
weather parameters as temperature, humidity, chances of rain, and speed of the wind.  
The attribute enables the farmers to plan farming operations (planting, irrigation and harvesting) 
depending on the weather.  
AI Advisory Module:  
The Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG) with a fine -tuned language model is used to 
employ an intelligent agricultural advisory module.  
Agricultural documents like crop guide, agricultural manuals, government agricultural resource 
and research articles are used to form a knowledge base. These documents are transformed into 
vector embedding and stored as a vector database that can be either  FAISS or ChromaDB.  
In case a farmer makes a query, the system carries out the following steps:  
1. The query is transformed into a vector representation.  
2. The most relevant agricultural documents are retrieved in the vector database.  
3. The information retrieved is then forwarded to a language model, which is narrowly 
focused.  
4. The language model is trained with agricultural data to enhance its capability of 
comprehending the language of the farm, and the model produces correct answers.  
5. The reasoning capability of the language model together with the knowledge that it 
retrieved is used as the final response. With this, the system will give sound and context -
sensitive agricultural advice.  
Response created is fed back to the mobile application and shown to the farmer. The text to 
speech system can also be used to convert the response into speech.  
Marketplace Module:  
The marketplace module enables farmers to have direct access to the agricultural suppliers. The 
application allows farmers to browse products including seeds, fertilizers, and pesticides and has 
---PAGE---
an opportunity to communicate with suppliers. This aspect minimizes the use of middlemen and 
assists farmers to acquire farming products at reasonable prices.  
Query Handling Portal:  
The query portal enables the farmers to pose queries concerning crop diseases, irrigation issues 
and methods of farming. Farmers can make queries either through text, voice or images.  
The AI advisory module receives these queries or passes them to agricultural experts. Answers 
are logged in a knowledge base in such a way that answers to similar questions should be made 
more effective in future.

5.6 Challenges During Implementation : 
The implementation of the PakFasal system had several challenges that were faced. A primary 
issue was to create a user interface that would be appropriate to the farmers who had low literacy 
levels. This problem was resolved through making the interface si mple with the help of icons and 
multilingualism as well as voice interaction.  
The second issue  is the integration of the IoT sensors and the cloud infrastructure. To have stable 
communication between ESP32 microcontrollers and Firebase, it was necessary to make network 
protocols and data synchronization systems properly configured.  
The offline functionality was also hard to deal with, since in most of the rural places in Pakistan, 
there is erratic internet connectivity. In order to address this issue, the local storage systems were 
introduced to allow access to important agricultural  content even in the absence of internet 
connection.  
The use of the RAG -based AI advisory module also was not without problems. Agricultural 
datasets must be prepared by cleaning, preprocess, and formatting the data to be used in training. 
Language models needed computation and attention to the parameters to  fine-tune.  
The other challenge was how to incorporate the database of vectors with the language model to 
provide a quick search of the relevant documents as well as the appropriate generation of 
responses.  
Nonetheless, these issues have been overcome as the PakFasal system has been fully deployed as 
a complete platform, which combines mobile technology, artificial intelligence, and IoT sensing 
to become a proponent of smart and sustainable agriculture.
